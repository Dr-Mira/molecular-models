Methods & Notes

Methods

The models were generated using a custom Python script. All atomic XYZ coordinates were calculated with the Merck Molecular Force Field (MMFF) [1]. The van der Waals atomic radii were adopted from the 2013 publication by Alvarez, "A cartography of the van der Waals territories" [2].

The bond order is modeled in such a way that the model represents the major resonance contributor. The model was visually verified and colored to comply with the corresponding PubChem database entry [3]. Support structures were also rigorously tested to ensure their easy removal.

About Force Fields

Force fields are mathematical models used to describe interactions between atoms and molecules. They are based on empirical parameters that represent the forces and energies associated with different types of atomic interactions, such as bond stretching, angle bending, and non-bonded interactions. These parameters are derived from experimental data and quantum mechanical calculations.

Printing Notes

  • Material: Optimized/tested for print with Bambu PLA and Elegoo PLA+.
  • Style: Ball-and-stick type (easiest to color, rigid, aesthetic).
  • Orientation: Oriented for optimal strength/waste/time compromise. Avoid placing critical bonds along the Z-axis to prevent breakage.
  • Supports: Removable with care (scalpel + sanding). Suggest using PETG as support interface (Heatbed 70°C) or PVA for best results.
